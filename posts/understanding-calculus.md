# Understanding Calculus

Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change. Let's explore its two main branches.

## Derivatives

The derivative of a function $f(x)$ at a point $x$ is defined as:

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$$

For example, if $f(x) = x^2$, then $f'(x) = 2x$.

## Integrals

The definite integral represents the area under a curve:

$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx$$

The fundamental theorem of calculus connects derivatives and integrals:

$$\int_a^b f'(x) \, dx = f(b) - f(a)$$

## Applications

Calculus has countless applications in physics, engineering, economics, and beyond. Understanding these concepts opens doors to deeper mathematical exploration.
